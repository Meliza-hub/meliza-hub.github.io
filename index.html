Cappuccino

A cappuccino is an espresso-based drink with equal parts of espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam. It has a stronger coffee flavor than a latte but is creamier than plain espresso. Often, it is topped with cocoa powder or cinnamon.

Expresso

Espresso is a concentrated form of coffee made by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee beans under high pressure. It has a strong, rich flavor and serves as the base for many drinks like lattes, cappuccinos, and macchiatos.

Latte

A latte is an espresso-based drink made with one shot of espresso and plenty of steamed milk, topped with a thin layer of milk foam. It has a smooth, creamy taste and is often flavored with syrups.
